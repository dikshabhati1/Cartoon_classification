\begin{frame}{Projectile Motion}
\begin{block}{What is Projectile}

A projectile is any object thrown by the exertion of a force.It can also be defined as object launched into the space an allowed to move free under the influence of gravity and air resistance.
\end{block}
\begin{block}{What is Projectile motion}
Projectile motion is a form of motion experienced by an object or particle (a projectile) that is projected near the earth's surface and moves along a curve path under the action of gravity only (in particular,the effects of air resistance are assumed to be negligible).
\end{block}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Projectile Motion}

The velocity V(t) of the projectile can  be obtained by integrating A(t) :\\
$$V(t)=\int{A}(t)dt=\int{(-g\textbf{j})}dt=-gt\textbf{j}+ C$$\\
where $C=V(t)$ is the velocity when t=0 (i.e. the initial velocity).Because the projectile is fired with initial speed 
$v_0=|V(0)|$ at an angle of a elevation $\alpha$ , the initial velocity must be\\
$$C_1=\textbf{V(0)}=v_0\cos\alpha\textbf{i}+v_0\sin\alpha\textbf{j}$$\\
and by substitution we find  
$$V(t)=-gt\textbf{j}+(v_0\cos\alpha\textbf){i}+(v_0\sin\alpha\textbf){j}$$
\end{frame}
\begin{frame}{Projectile Motion}
$$V(t)=(v_0\cos\alpha)\textbf{i}+(v_0\sin\alpha-gt)\textbf{j}$$\\
Next, by integrating \textbf{V} with respect to t, we find that the position vector of the projectile is
$$\textbf{R}(t)=\int{V(t)}dt=\int[(v_0\cos\alpha)\textbf{i}+(v_0\sin\alpha-gt)\textbf{j}]dt$$
$$=(v_0\cos\alpha)t\textbf{i}+[(v_0\sin\alpha)t-\frac{1}{2}gt^2]\textbf{j}+C_0$$
where $C_2=R(0)$ is the position at time t=0.
\end{frame}
